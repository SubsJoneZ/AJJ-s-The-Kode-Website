Top 3 Reasons to Collaborate

1) ENHANCE YOUR PRODUCTIVITY: Companies thrive on the collaborative efforts of bright individuals. The ability to bring skills and ideas together is how start-ups are formed. Did you know that the biggest and most profitable tech giants known to man started with a conversation in a common place... a school, a place of business, a birthday party, etc? Networking doesn't have to be this big event. Sometimes all we have to do is commit to working together nomatter how small the project. Synergy is guaranteed to produce something great. Statistically, synergy is undefeated. Connecting with a team of developers will enhance your productivity and your profits.

2) If starting your own company is the reason you take interest in coding, then you definitely will benefit from knowing someone who has the general knowledge of how digital marketing works for businesses. Having a connection to web developers is the ultimate advantage any business can have. They can teach you about SEO (Search Engine Optimization) and help you implement it to increase traffic and sales. If you need someone to build an app or website for your business, and/or teach you how to do it yourself, you can't lose by reaching out to someone in our community. Sign up and we can touch bases.

3) Imagine having millions of mentors. On the internet, you do. In web development, most of the work is already done for you. It's like an open book test. Most of the problems you will encounter are already figured out. Since the best things in life don't come easy, you will benefit from the perplexities of this journey. Your ability to find logic will greatly be enhanced with each project. And, you will always have the tech gods to refer to for answers to lead you in the right direction. While building this project, each day I searched "how to" before receiving information provided by at least 85% of the entire tech community, from the Americas, India, Canada, Europe, Africa, Australia, Japan, etc. Even those who struck it rich in tech still share tips and best practices online to assist us in taking it further. Next time you search Google, remember that it is a giant complex in California that pays it's 140,000 employees a top tier salary to send us valuable information on the web for free. I'm not an expert, I taught myself to be a fullstack developer and I can teach you. If you'll like to get started on your journey, go to the Contact section on the home page and hit me up. Let's do some real work and have fun. Let's collab!
